# Resume Best Practices Guide

Industry-standard resume writing practices for professional job seekers.

---

## 1. Layout & Format

### Page Length
- **2 pages maximum** for 10+ years experience
- **1 page** for less than 10 years experience
- **Why:** Recruiters spend 6-7 seconds on initial scan

### Font & Styling
- **Calibri or Cambria** 10-11pt
- Mix left-justified and centered elements
- Color/designed templates are acceptable (but keep ATS-friendly)

### Address
- ✅ **Leave off street address** (e.g., don't include "123 Main Street")
- ✅ **Include city, state, zip** (e.g., "[YOUR_CITY, STATE] 02101")
- ✅ Include phone and email
- ✅ Include LinkedIn profile URL

### Margins
- Recommended: 0.5-1 inch all sides
- Tighter margins acceptable if needed for 2-page limit (minimum 0.4")

## 2. Content Structure

### Top 1/3 Most Important

The top third of your resume is prime real estate. Include:

1. **Name** (large, centered or left-aligned)
2. **Headline** (strong and descriptive)
3. **Professional Summary** (3-4 sentences)
4. **Areas of Expertise / Core Competencies** (keywords)

### Headline Examples

**Strong headlines:**
- "Digital Marketing Manager for Tech Startups"
- "IT Leader | Cloud Infrastructure & Cybersecurity Operations"
- "Identity Management Architect"
- "Strategic IT Director | Enterprise Systems & Team Development"

**Weak headlines:**
- "Experienced Professional" (too vague)
- "IT Manager" (too generic)

**Purpose:** Immediately tell recruiters what you do and where you add value.

### Professional Summary

**Format:**
- 3-4 sentences
- Under 80 words
- Emphasize years of experience, key skills, and areas of impact

**Example:**
> Strategic IT leader with 15+ years of progressive experience driving cloud modernization, identity governance, and automation at scale. Expert in balancing hands-on technical execution with strategic leadership across systems management, security operations, and help desk delivery. Proven track record of building and mentoring distributed international teams while implementing compliance programs and cloud-first infrastructure.

### Areas of Expertise / Core Competencies

**Format:**
- Keyword-rich section
- 4-6 categories or bullet lists
- Complements professional summary

**Purpose:**
- ATS keyword matching
- Quick-scan for recruiters
- Shows breadth of knowledge

**Example:**
```
Identity Management: Enterprise IdP, SSO, SCIM, MFA, Conditional Access
Cloud Infrastructure: Cloud Platform, Enterprise SaaS Platform, Datacenter Migration
Automation: Scripting, Workflow Automation Platform, CI/CD Pipelines
Leadership: Team Development, Budget Management, Strategic Planning
```

## 3. Experience Bullets

### Quantity Guidelines

**Current role:** 5-7 bullets MAX
**Previous roles:** 3-5 bullets each
**Older roles:** 2-3 bullets or just job description

**Why fewer bullets?**
- Visual balance for scanning
- Forces you to select most relevant achievements
- Prevents overwhelming the reader

### Bullet Format: What + How + Impact

**Structure:**
1. **What** you did (action verb)
2. **How** you did it (methods, tools, approach)
3. **Impact** (quantified results when possible)

**Examples:**

✅ **Good:**
> Led enterprise-wide EDR Solution deployment across 500+ endpoints, integrating with Directory Service and Enterprise IdP for comprehensive security monitoring and reducing security incidents by 40%

- **What:** Led EDR Solution deployment
- **How:** Across 500+ endpoints, integrated with AD/Enterprise IdP
- **Impact:** Reduced security incidents by 40%

✅ **Good:**
> Designed employee onboarding automations utilizing SCIM and SSO, reducing manual IT workload by 70% while improving security posture

- **What:** Designed onboarding automations
- **How:** Using SCIM and SSO
- **Impact:** 70% reduction in manual work, improved security

❌ **Weak:**
> Responsible for managing IT systems

- **What:** Managing IT systems (too vague)
- **How:** Not specified
- **Impact:** Not quantified

### Success Language - Action Verbs

**Use strong action verbs:**
- Led, Managed, Directed, Oversaw
- Spearheaded, Championed, Drove
- Improved, Streamlined, Optimized, Modernized
- Achieved, Delivered, Completed
- Implemented, Deployed, Built, Architected
- Accelerated, Transformed, Overhauled, Revamped

**Avoid weak verbs:**
- Responsible for, Tasked with, Worked on
- Helped, Assisted (unless that's truly your role)

### Category Headers: Context Matters

**Industry standard:** "NO sub-bullets or category headers"

**BUT - This is contextual based on role level:**

#### ❌ Remove Category Headers For:
- **Individual contributor roles** (specialist, engineer, analyst)
- **Narrow-focus positions** (all work is in same domain)
- **Entry to mid-level roles** (simplicity aids scanning)
- **Tight space constraints** (need every line for content)

**Why:** Keeps visual hierarchy simple, maximizes space for achievements

**Example - Specialist Role (NO categories):**
```markdown
### Identity Management Specialist
Company | Location | Dates

- Led enterprise-wide SSO migration...
- Architected federated guest access...
- Implemented SCIM provisioning automation...
- Deployed privileged access management...
```

#### ✅ Keep Category Headers For:
- **Director/VP level roles** (shows strategic organization)
- **Complex multi-domain roles** (Security + Infrastructure + Operations)
- **Leadership positions** (managing multiple functional areas)
- **Comprehensive resumes** (LinkedIn, networking)

**Why:** Demonstrates organizational thinking, breadth of responsibility, strategic leadership

**Example - Director Role (WITH categories):**
```markdown
### [Your Job Title] / Director
Company | Location | Dates

Security & Identity Management
- Led enterprise-wide SSO migration...
- Architected federated access...

Infrastructure Modernization & Automation
- Completed datacenter retirement...
- Implemented event-driven HR automation...

Operations & Compliance
- Managed distributed international team...
- Maintained PCI and SOX compliance programs...
```

#### Guidelines for Category Headers:

**Use 3-5 category headers MAX** (not a bullet under each category)
- Group 2-3 related bullets per category
- Use bold formatting for category names
- No sub-bullets (bullets under bullets) - categories are organizational only

**Category names should be:**
- Descriptive of functional area (not vague)
- Industry-standard terminology
- Aligned with job description domains

**Decision Framework:**
1. **Role level?** Director+ → Consider categories | IC/Specialist → No categories
2. **Bullet count?** 10+ bullets → Categories help organization | <7 bullets → Skip categories
3. **Domain diversity?** Multiple distinct areas → Categories show scope | Single focus → Skip categories
4. **Target audience?** Leadership hiring panel → Categories work | ATS optimization → Skip categories

## 4. Quantifying Achievements

### Why Quantify?

Numbers make achievements concrete and credible:
- "Managed IT for 300+ employees across 20 offices"
- "Reduced manual workload by 70%"
- "Led migration of [NUMBER] [REGION]-based employees"
- "Managed team of 5 personnel and $500K budget"

### What to Quantify

- Team size
- Number of users/employees supported
- Budget managed
- Percentage improvements (time saved, costs reduced)
- Number of systems/devices/locations
- Project timelines (ahead of schedule, on time)

### When You Don't Have Numbers

Focus on scope and impact:
- "Enterprise-wide deployment" (vs. just "deployment")
- "Global multi-tenant environment" (shows scale)
- "Distributed international team" (shows complexity)

## 5. Tailoring for Each Job

### Research the Company

Before customizing:
1. Read job description carefully - note required skills
2. Research company challenges (news, blog posts, LinkedIn)
3. Identify industry-specific terminology
4. Look for culture fit indicators

### Incorporate Job Description Terms

**Natural integration:**
- Use exact phrases from job description where they match your experience
- Don't force keywords where they don't fit
- Maintain authentic voice

**Example:**
- **Job says:** "Experience with zero-trust security architecture"
- **Your bullet:** "Implemented zero-trust principles through Elevate deployment and conditional access policies"

### What to Emphasize

Based on job requirements, reorder and emphasize:
- **Summary:** Highlight most relevant experience first
- **Competencies:** Put matching categories first
- **Bullets:** Select bullets that align with job needs

## 6. AI Usage Guidelines

### ✅ AI CAN Help With:

- Aligning your capabilities with job requirements
- Integrating keywords naturally
- Proofreading grammar and spelling
- Suggesting alternative phrasing
- Reformatting content

### ❌ AI CANNOT Replace:

- **Checking content for accuracy** - YOU must verify all facts
- **Fine-tuning new content** - YOU must ensure it matches your voice
- **Ensuring authentic voice** - AI-generated text often sounds generic

### Best Practices:

1. Start with your own content (base master)
2. Use AI to refine, not create from scratch
3. Verify EVERY claim AI suggests
4. Ensure it sounds like you, not a robot

**Industry guidance:**
> "AI is a tool to help align and refine, but YOU are responsible for accuracy and authenticity."

## 8. Education Section

### What to Include

- **Degree first, area of study second**
  - Example: "Associate's Degree in Business Administration"
- **School name and location**
- **Graduation date is NOT necessary** (especially if it reveals age)

### Additional Items

If space permits:
- Professional development courses
- Relevant certifications
- Volunteer leadership roles
- Awards and recognition

### When to Minimize

If you have 15+ years experience:
- Education can be brief (just degree, school, no details)
- Focus space on professional experience instead

## 9. Technical Skills / Tools

### Only Include Current Tools

**Industry standard:**
- ❌ Don't list tools you used 10 years ago and haven't touched since
- ✅ Include tools you use currently or recently
- ✅ Include tools you're proficient in and would use in next role

### Organization

**Option 1: Integrate into competencies**
```
Identity Management: Enterprise IdP, SSO (SAML/OIDC), SCIM, MFA
```

**Option 2: Separate Technical Skills section**
```
Technical Skills: Cloud Platform, MDM Platform, MDM Solution, EDR Solution, Scripting, Git
```

### Avoid Skill Ratings

❌ Don't do: "Scripting ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Expert)"
✅ Instead: List it in competencies or technical skills without rating

**Why:** Skill ratings are subjective and don't add value.

## 10. ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Compatibility

### What is ATS?

90% of companies use software to:
- Parse resumes into database fields
- Search for keywords matching job requirements
- Rank candidates before human review

### ATS-Friendly Formatting

✅ **DO:**
- Use standard fonts (Calibri, Cambria, Arial)
- Use standard section headers (Professional Experience, Education)
- Save as .docx (most compatible)
- Use simple bullets (•)
- Left-align text
- Include keywords from job description

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use tables, columns, or text boxes
- Put important info in headers/footers
- Use images or graphics
- Use special characters excessively
- Use unusual section headers
- Save as PDF (unless specifically requested)

### Testing ATS Compatibility

**Jobscan test:**
1. Upload .docx to Jobscan
2. Check parsing accuracy
3. Fix any formatting issues flagged

**Manual test:**
1. Copy/paste resume into plain text editor
2. If content is garbled or out of order, ATS may struggle too

## 11. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Mistakes

❌ **Listing duties instead of achievements**
- Bad: "Responsible for managing IT infrastructure"
- Good: "Led IT infrastructure modernization, completing datacenter retirement 6 months ahead of schedule"

❌ **Using clichés and buzzwords**
- Avoid: "Results-oriented team player who thinks outside the box"
- Better: Specific achievements that demonstrate these qualities

❌ **Including irrelevant experience**
- If it doesn't support the job you're applying for, consider removing it

❌ **Being dishonest**
- Don't exaggerate titles, duration, or scope
- Don't claim skills you don't have
- Background checks WILL verify employment

### Formatting Mistakes

❌ **Inconsistent formatting**
- Pick a style and stick with it (dates, bullets, spacing)

❌ **Walls of text**
- Break up long paragraphs
- Use bullet points for easy scanning

❌ **Too much information**
- More is not better - curate to most relevant content
